Frequently Asked Questions

Answers, in plain English

Common questions about fixed term deposits and how SecureYield works.

What is a fixed term deposit? +
A fixed term deposit is a savings product where you lock away a sum of money with a bank or credit union for a set period (the "term"), in exchange for a guaranteed interest rate. Terms typically range from one month to five years. Your principal and interest are returned at maturity.
Are my deposits protected? +
In Australia, deposits with APRA-authorised banks, credit unions, and building societies are covered by the Financial Claims Scheme up to $250,000 per account-holder per institution. SecureYield only lists APRA-authorised institutions, so this protection applies to every rate in our table.
Does SecureYield hold my money? +
No. SecureYield is a comparison service — we never receive, hold, or transfer customer funds. When you proceed with a deposit, your money goes directly to the issuing institution, where it is held in your name.
How does SecureYield make money? +
Some institutions pay us a small referral fee when a customer opens an account through our platform. These fees do not affect the order in which rates appear, nor are they passed on to you. We disclose any commercial relationships clearly on each listing.
Can I withdraw my deposit early? +
Most fixed term deposits allow early withdrawal but with conditions — typically 31 days' notice and an interest rate reduction. The specifics vary by institution and product, so we recommend reading the Product Disclosure Statement before committing your funds.
Do you provide financial advice? +
No. SecureYield provides general factual information about products from APRA-authorised institutions. We are not licensed to provide personal financial advice. If you need advice tailored to your circumstances, please consult a licensed financial adviser.
